History of Ruby

February 24, 1993: Development started on Ruby
 - Yukihiro Matsumoto and Keiju Ishitsuka work together to start developing Ruby
December 21st, 1995: ruby-0.95 released
December 25, 1996: ruby-1.0 released
July 1997: Matsumoto hired by netlab.jp to develop Ruby full time
August 13th, 1997: ruby-1.1 released
Sept. 22nd, 1997: First article about Ruby published (lost media)
 -Popularity of Ruby begins to grow in Japan
2000: Ruby becomes more popular than Python in Japan
2004: Ruby on Rails launches
2006: Ruby voted Language of the Year by TIOBE
2006: Ruby achieves “mass acceptance”
 -Active user groups in major cities around the world
 -Ruby related conferences begin to fill to capacity
 -Mailing list for Ruby discussion climbed to an average of 200 messages per day
2013: Ruby 2.0.0 released
2016: Ruby reaches the highest position in the TIOBE index yet at number 8
2020: Ruby 3.0.0 released
End of 2022: 2.4 million Ruby developers
April 14th, 2025: The latest stable version of Ruby (3.4.3) was released
(Stabile, 2023)
